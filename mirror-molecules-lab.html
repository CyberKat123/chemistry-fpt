R/S Naming System

To distinguish between molecules, scientists use the Cahn-Ingold-Prelog (R/S) naming system.

This system was established by chemists R.S. Cahn, C. Ingold, and V. Prelog, and it uses three rules to unambiguously determine the absolute configuration of chiral centers within molecules.

  1. List the four substituents around the chiral center in descending order of atomic number.
  2. Orient the molecule so the lowest substituent is at the back.
  3. Draw an arrow connecting numbers one, two, and three. Counterclockwise is S; clockwise is R.

In the event of a tie, move to the next atom in the chain and continue ranking until a difference is found.

Importance of Distinguishing Between Molecules

The difference between left-handed and right-handed molecules becomes incredibly important in pharmacology. More than half of all drugs currently used pharmaceutically are chiral, with 90% of those drugs being racemic.

This is significant, as the enzymes, proteins, and receptors in the body interact with each enantiomer differently.

Eutomer

The bioactive enantiomer that delivers the desired effect.

Distomer

The opposite, which may reduce efficacy, require chiral inversion, or cause toxicity and undesired side effects.

Case Studies

The Thalidomide Tragedy

In the late 1950s/early 1960s, thalidomide was prescribed in a racemic mixture to pregnant women as a way to treat morning sickness. The right-handed molecule was a safe and effective sedative, but the left-handed one was highly toxic and resulted in severe birth defects in thousands of infants across the globe.

L-DOPA vs. D-DOPA

While developing treatment for Parkinson's disease, scientists prescribed a racemic mixture of L-dopa and D-dopa. D-dopa was highly ineffective and caused dangerous granulocytopenia. Pure L-dopa is now the clinical standard.

Ethambutol

(S,S)-Ethambutol has been able to cure Mycobacterium tuberculosis and save thousands of lives. (R,R)-ethambutol has a fraction of the potency while increasing the risk of optic neuropathy.

It’s evident how mirrored molecules can affect lives when used properly and improperly. But what if every part of an entire organism were flipped?
