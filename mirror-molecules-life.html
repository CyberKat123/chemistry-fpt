Magnetic Minerals

Various studies suggest that homochirality may have been created or amplified as a result of the magnetic minerals found on Earth. Experimental research discovered that the magnetic fields from magnetite, a common mineral on Earth, are biased towards a certain enantiomer of RAO (an important precursor of the RNA molecule). This result is theorized to be caused by the chiral-induced spin selectivity (C.I.S.S.) effect, which causes electron spins to interact differently in the presence of magnetic materials depending on molecule handedness. Once RAO began to accumulate, it formed crystalline structures that amplified the bias (a phenomenon known as amplification), resulting in a mixture with nearly pure chirality.

Extraterrestrial Delivery

One of the most popular scientific theories for the origin of life on Earth is the delivery of organic compounds from a meteor, and this theory could also account for homochirality. Evidence such as the Murchison meteorite shows that meteorites can have more of one chirality of an organic molecule than the other. This means that meteors could have delivered an abundance of a certain enantiomer, which was slowly amplified over time until homochirality was achieved.

Circularly Polarized Light

Certain star-forming regions emit circularly polarized ultraviolet light, which can destroy one enantiomer of a compound, resulting in an abundance of the other. A similar effect was observed in the lab in a 2009 experiment where a racemic slurry of amino acid derivatives into pure acetonitrile as a result of polarized light. Scientists theorize this, in combination with amplification, could be a factor in the homochirality of life on Earth.
