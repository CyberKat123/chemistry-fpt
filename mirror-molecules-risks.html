Life Saving or World Ending?

↓

Potential Benefits

Mirror cells could have a variety of unique interactions with natural cells and living things. These properties could be used to benefit medicine, agriculture, and many other fields.

Taking Advantage of Handedness

Hundreds of products we consume utilize fermentation. A main risk is contamination by phages, a type of virus that consumes bacteria. Mirror bacteria could evade phages and solve countless problems in manufacturing food, drinks, and medicine.

A New World of Medicines

Mirror peptides have the potential to avoid being broken down by the body's natural processes while still binding to the correct target cells, allowing therapeutics with longer efficacy, fewer side effects, and a lower dose.

Potential Risks

Despite the potential benefits, teams of specialists across the world are arguing against the pursuit of mirror life, warning that it could possibly lead to irreversible ecosystem destruction or the creation of untreatable diseases.

Unstoppable Invasive Species

If mirror bacteria were synthesized and released, they would have no predators to keep their population under control, allowing them to rapidly replicate and consume resources with no way to stop them. This imbalance would destroy natural microorganism populations and ecosystems.

Undetectable, Incurable Diseases

Scientists hypothesize that mirror bacteria would go undetected by immune systems and would not respond to antibiotics. This poses a risk for diseases with unprecedented mortality and possible chemical warfare.

Whether positive or negative, the development of mirror life holds the potential to make massive change in our world.
